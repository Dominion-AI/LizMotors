Enternal Market Research

External market research refers to the process of gathering information and insights about the market from sources outside of the organization conducting the research. This type of research involves collecting data and analyzing trends, preferences, behaviours, and other relevant factors.

Customers:

Gatherings feedback, conducting surveys, and analyzing purchasing patterns of existing and potential customers.

Competitors:

Studying the strategies, products, pricing, and marketing efforts of competitors in the same industry or market.

Industry Reports and Publications:

Reviewing reports, articles, and studies published by industry analysts, research firms, and trade associations.

Government data & Experts opinions:

Government data
Accessing demographic, economic, and regulatory information provided by government agencies.

Expert opinions
Consulting with industry experts, consultants, and advisors to gain valuable insights and perspectives.

Market trends:

Tracking consumer trends, technological advancements, and other market developments that could impact the industry.

In Conclusion;

External market research helps organiztions understandthe external envionment in which they operate, identify opportunity and threats, assess market demand, and make informed decisions about product development, marketing strategies, and business expansion.
